Looking Forward To The Road

What is autonomous electric bus services?

Autonomous bus services are transportation systems where self-driving buses operate without human drivers. These buses are programmed to navigate predefined routes, pick up and drop off passengers, and ensure safety without human intervention.

While the buses can navigate routes and perform basic functions autonomously, there are human operators or supervisors present to monitor the system, intervene if necessary, and ensure passenger safety.

THE GENESIS OF EMOOVIT

eMoovit Technology Sdn Bhd was founded in 2016 as a Malaysian tech company specializing in developing autonomous driving solutions. The company creates vehicle-agnostic driverless software, meaning their technology can be applied to various types of vehicles. From the start, eMoovit focused on autonomous mobility solutions for urban transportation, such as self-driving cars, electric buses, and last-mile logistics.

The company gained prominence by becoming the first in Malaysia to receive approval for testing autonomous vehicles on public roads in Cyberjaya. This was a significant step in Malaysia’s efforts to build infrastructure for autonomous driving, with eMoovit playing a key role in shaping the country's future in this space.

MOVING TO FUTURISE

eMoovit Technology, in partnership with Futurise, moved its operations to Cyberjaya, Malaysia, to participate in the country's autonomous vehicle initiatives. Futurise, a government-linked subsidiary under the Ministry of Finance, collaborated with eMoovit to establish Malaysia’s first self-driving vehicle test route. This test route, called the Malaysia Autonomous Vehicle (MyAV) Testing Route, was designed to support the development and testing of autonomous vehicles on public roads.

eMoovit’s move to Futurise signifies its commitment to advancing autonomous vehicle technology in Malaysia, leveraging the infrastructure and collaboration opportunities in Cyberjaya​

FIRST AV PROTOTYPE

eMoovit Technology's first autonomous vehicle (AV) prototype was based on a modified Proton Exora, a popular Malaysian MPV (multi-purpose vehicle). This prototype was designed to test the capabilities of their driverless software on public roads. The Proton Exora was equipped with advanced sensors and eMoovit's self-driving technology, allowing it to navigate autonomously in controlled environments.

The development of this prototype marked a significant milestone for the company as it became the first to receive approval to test autonomous vehicles on the Malaysia Autonomous Vehicle (MyAV) Testing Route in Cyberjaya. This testing route, established with the help of Futurise, provided eMoovit with a platform to refine its technology for future applications in urban transportation and logistics.

FIRST DEBUT IN NATIONAL MEDIA

eMoovit Technology made its first significant debut in national media around 2019 and 2020 when it became the first company in Malaysia to receive approval for testing autonomous vehicles on public roads in Cyberjaya. This breakthrough was widely covered in national outlets like Free Malaysia Today and WargaBiz, highlighting the company's efforts in developing autonomous driving technology and its collaboration with Futurise to create the Malaysia Autonomous Vehicle (MyAV) Testing Route​

The media also focused on eMoovit's Proton Exora, their first autonomous vehicle prototype, as it became a symbol of Malaysia’s push towards self-driving vehicle innovation. The coverage emphasized the potential for autonomous systems to transform urban mobility and logistics in the country.

ONE MONTH TRIAL

eMoovit Technology conducted a one-month trial of their autonomous vehicle in Cyberjaya as part of the initial testing phase on the Malaysia Autonomous Vehicle (MyAV) Testing Route. The trial involved using their autonomous Proton Exora, equipped with their driverless software, to navigate through controlled public routes. This trial allowed eMoovit to collect data, refine their software, and ensure safety compliance.

During the one-month period, the company tested various scenarios, including urban traffic conditions, and monitored the vehicle's performance to improve its autonomous capabilities for future deployments

PUBLIC TESTING

eMoovit Technology began public testing of its autonomous vehicle in Cyberjaya after receiving approval from Malaysian authorities. This testing was carried out on the Malaysia Autonomous Vehicle (MyAV) Testing Route, a designated area for evaluating self-driving vehicles on public roads. The trials were significant as they marked the first time a Malaysian company had legally tested autonomous vehicles in public, using a modified Proton Exora equipped with eMoovit's vehicle-agnostic driverless software.

The public tests aimed to assess the vehicle's performance in real-world urban environments, ensuring safety and gathering crucial data for further software improvements. The route covered about 7 kilometers around the Futurise campus in Cyberjaya, with a safety officer always on board to oversee the trials

AUTONOMOUS MODE

In autonomous mode, eMoovit Technology's self-driving vehicles, such as their modified Proton Exora, operate without human intervention. The vehicle relies on a combination of sensors, cameras, and their proprietary driverless software to navigate through urban environments. These vehicles are capable of detecting obstacles, interpreting traffic conditions, and making real-time driving decisions, all while ensuring passenger safety.

During the testing on public roads, the vehicle would switch into autonomous mode within the designated MyAV Testing Route in Cyberjaya. However, for safety, a trained safety officer was always present in the vehicle to monitor its performance and intervene if necessary​.

New Product Release

eMoovit Technology has been involved in developing autonomous electric buses as part of Malaysia’s push toward sustainable and smart urban mobility. Their autonomous electric bus project is designed for public transport and aims to address first/last-mile transportation solutions. The bus operates using eMoovit's vehicle-agnostic driverless software, enabling it to navigate through urban areas without human intervention.

In collaboration with Futurise and under the MyAV Testing Route in Cyberjaya, eMoovit has been testing these autonomous electric buses to ensure they meet safety and operational standards. The buses are also expected to integrate 5G technology to enhance real-time communication and improve route efficiency.

The project aligns with global trends of adopting electric and autonomous vehicles to reduce carbon emissions and traffic congestion in cities​.
